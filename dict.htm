Hacker Dictionary

Hackers use special words to talk about computers so that idiots that listen to their conversations won't report them to the police. I've spent 2 years learning all of the hacker words and I'm going to share them with you. The hacker code normally prevents people form sharing information but I'm a black hat hacker so I do whatever I want.

Words

Computer Network
A bunch of computers linked together so they can communicate and share information.
Internet
A collection of computer networks on a local, regional, national, or even international level. Put simply, the Internet is just a network of networks.
MODEM
A device that lets your computer communicate over a phone line.
Nerd
Someone who's life is focused on computers and technology.
Geek
Someone who's life is focused on computers and technology and likes it that way.
Internaut
Someone who travels the Internet, boldly going where millions of people have gone before.
Cyberspace
A term invented by a science fiction writer to describe a fictional environment where computers and people live and work together. On the Internet cyberspace means roughly the same thing. It includes the computers, the network connections, and the software and computer data that make up the Net.
E-mail
A message sent on a computer.
SPAM
Spam means sending thousands of copies of the same message all over Usenet.
Flaming
Unusually pointless and excessive outrage in electronic mail.
Newbies
New comers to the Internet.
Gopherspace
The total of all the information you can get by using Gopher. Since Gopher is a very popular service it is a huge and expanding part of the Internet.
Download
Copying information or a program from a remote computer to your own.
Hypertext
Hypertext documents linked to one another by keywords or graphic images. Any time you come to a key word you can jump to another document with additional information on that subject. The new document will also have hypertext links so you can search for information in an interactive manner.
The number of hypertext documents available on the Net is still somewhat limited so you may find more on the subject in a Gopher search.
